Data Security

1. Introduction to Data Security

Data Security means putting protective measures in place to keep a database safe from hackers, accidental deletions, or system crashes.

Common Causes of Database Damage

  • Human Mistakes & Actions: Hackers stealing data, or employees accidentally deleting files.
  • Physical Disasters: Fire, flooding, earthquakes, or someone physically stealing the servers.
  • Hardware & Power Failures: Power outages, sudden voltage drops, or hard drives breaking down.

2. Importance of Data Security

Protecting Secrets (Intellectual Property)

Keeping a company's unique formulas or designs safe so competitors cannot copy them.

Example: If the secret recipe for Coca-Cola leaked online, anyone could make it, destroying the company's business edge.

Saving the Brand's Reputation

Making sure customers feel safe doing business with a company.

Example: If a major social media app leaks user passwords, millions of people will close their accounts and switch to a competitor.

Avoiding Heavy Financial Costs

Fixing a data breach is incredibly expensive.

Example: After a breach, a bank must spend millions of dollars to hire cybersecurity experts, update software, pay legal fines, and compensate affected customers.
