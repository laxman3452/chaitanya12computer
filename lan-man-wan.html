Computer Network Types (Exam Prep)

1. Local Area Network (LAN)

A Local Area Network (LAN) is a network that interconnects computers and devices within a limited geographical area, such as a single room, office, home, or a building.

Key Characteristics

  • Coverage: Small geographic area (usually less than 1–2 km).
  • Ownership: Private (owned by a single person or organization).
  • Speed: Very high data transmission speeds (ranging from 100 Mbps to several Gbps).

Advantages

  • High Speed: Data transmission is faster compared to MAN and WAN.
  • Low Cost: It is cheaper to set up, manage, and maintain.
  • High Security: Offers tighter security controls because it is isolated from the public network.

2. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

A Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) is a network that covers a larger geographic area than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, typically spanning an entire city or town. A prime example is a cable TV network.

Key Characteristics

  • Coverage: Medium geographic area (typically 5 km to 50 km).
  • Ownership: Can be private or public utilities.
  • Media: Frequently uses high-speed backbone lines such as fiber-optic cabling.

3. Wide Area Network (WAN)

A Wide Area Network (WAN) is a network that extends over a vast geographical distance, interconnecting computers across cities, states, countries, or even continents. The Internet is the most common example of a WAN.

Key Characteristics

  • Coverage: Large/Global geographic area (thousands of kilometers).
  • Devices Used: Utilizes advanced networking equipment like routers, gateways, switches, and satellite linkages.
  • Speed: Slower relative speeds with higher error rates due to distances involved.
